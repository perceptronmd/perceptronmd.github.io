Abnormal Uterine Bleeding

Posted on Jan 20, 2021

Encounter

CA 45 year-old G2P0020 woman, with LMP 21 days ago, presents with heavy
menstrual bleeding. Prior to 6 months ago her cycles occurred every 28-30 days,
lasted for 6 days, and were associated with cramps that were relieved
by Ibuprofen. In the last 6 months there has been a change with menses
occurring every 25-32 days, lasting 7-10 days and associated with cramps not
relieved by ibuprofen, passing clots and using two boxes of maxi pads each
cycle. She is worried about losing her job if the bleeding is not better
controlled. She denies dizziness, but complains of feeling weak
and fatigued. Her weight has not changed in the last year. She denies any
bleeding disorders or reproductive cancers in the family. She uses condoms
for contraception. She takes no daily medications and has no other medical problems.
She is married and works in a factory.

On physical exam, her weight is 150 pounds, height is 5 feet, 6 inches,
BP 130/88, P 100. She appears pale. Pelvic exam shows normal vulva,
vagina and cervix; normal sized, non-tender, mobile uterus; non-tender
adnexae without palpable masses.

1. What are the parameters of a normal menstrual cycle?

- 21 to 35 days, first to first
- <2 days flow=  oligomenorreha; 7+ days = menometrorrhagia
- Example
  - If 35 day cycle, ovulation happen at 35-14=21 day
    - Once ovulation happen there are two type of cysts:
      - Follicular cyst: has an egg, produces estrogen ONLY
      - Corpus luteum cyst: produces estrogen AND progesterone
        - Estrogen only = follicular phase
        - Estrogen and prog together = secretory phase
    - 14 day impt because corpus luteum can live 14 days

Other scenarios

  1. 16 year old without a cycle for 6 months, hirsutism, thick skin on neck (acanthosis nigricans), hair on belly, heavy, pelvic exam normal (uterus is normal size)
  • PCOS
    • Ovulatory dysfunction; will not see bigger uterus
    • Management = OCPs; can encourage weight loss but that takes longer time
  1. 16 yo regular cycles, when she has a cycle she is bleeding with huge clots, changing pad every hour, CBC = 6, normal size uterus
  • Workup
    • Coagulation studies
      • Von willebrand
  • Say she has enlarged uterus
    • Fibroids, adenomyosis (takes longer vs fibroids)
  1. 40 yo pt with abnl bleeding and enlarged uterus
  • Malignancy
    • Workup: endometrial bx
  1. 30 yo heavy bleeding, Hb low
  • Workup
    • Pregnancy test
      • If negative and has normal size uterus
        • Thyroid test
        • Ultrasound
          • If Fibroid
            • Myometomy
            • Or medical tx: OCP or triamateric acid
  1. 30 yo heavy bleeding, anemic, ultrasound normal, TSH/T3/T4 normal, normal size uterus

Workup coagulopathy

    - vWF
    - F8 deficiency (hemophilia A)
    - Platelet deficiency
    - Fibrinogen deficiency
    - acquired
      - Heparin induced
      - Liver disease
      - Taking blood thinners
      - Infection of uterus
      - Immune mediated - ITP, SLE, RA
      - Cancer - leukemia, lymphoma etc (WBC 40K)

General rule:

    - if pt <35-40 yo, never do a bx first, look for other systemic causes (1. PCOS, 2. Genetic or acquired coagulopathy)
    - If pt 40+
      - No atrophy unless pt in menopause (no cycles for 12 months)
      - Over 40 that is normal but can cause abnl bleeding
        - Perimenopause:
          - Decline of ovulatory function; it becomes estrogen dependent;
            - Normally progesterone cleans that up, but with unopposed estrogen there is more growth of uterine lining (like a lawn that grows but you dont have a lawnmower (progesterone))
          - Tx:
            - LNG IUD, cyclic progesterone, OCP
            - Surgery
              - Hysterectomy,
              - endometrial ablation,
              - myometomy,
              - embolization (IR does this)

  1. BP 80/60, HR 140, RR 30, very heavy active bleeding

    • IVF resuscitation
    • OR immediately => hysterectomy

  2. BP 120/80, HR 110, actively bleeding

    • IV estrogen OR high-dose OCP (estrogen)
      • When u use high-dose hormones they stop bleeding

  3. 16yo F, Hb 6, symptomatic, HDS, headache, weakness

    • Offer blood transfusion and she refuses
      • PO iron (IV doesn’t get the blood count up faster vs PO)
        • Takes 1 week for Hb to go up by 1
      • IMPORTANT: If a young patient getting transfusion, the pt can develop antibodies

  4. 40yo F, 80/40, actively bleeding, H/H 10/30

    • Even though Hb 10, you should transfuse now (Hb takes time to catch up; shes actively bleeding and symptomatic)

  5. 16 yo F

    • Bone marrow dysplasia
      • Retic count low, white white count = lymphoma, leukemia

      • Cirrhosis
        • High liver enzyme
      • Varices in stomach
      • Colon cancer
    • Genetic
      • Sickle cell
